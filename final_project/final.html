Process and Challenges

Data Collection and Data Cleaning

I think as a car-using heavy country, it is interesting to explore the way Americans choose to commute, especially among drive-alone, carpooling and public transportation. Knowing people's commute Preference within different states could be helpful no matter for building public goods in transportation or environmental protection. To get the access for these data, I went to some census dataset, such as The American Community Survey (ACS), Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED), Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS). I used ACS dataset as transportation options, carpool & number of riders) related data as our analysis source. Our census data mainly come from FRED, which allows us to use their API to retrieve population statistics statewide. Besides these two data sources, we decide to use BTS data to get transportation services within the U.S. by different states. However, since BTS did not conclude this data as an actual dataset, we could not actually download it directly from their dataset thus we used web scraping to grab their table.

The biggest challenge for me here, I would say, we all know the census data could be super detailed and messy. At first, cleaning a massive dataset was very time consuming. I used python to pre-cleaning my data to keep something I need. Secondly, when I look up the population census data for 2020, I realize 2020 is the year that COVID pandemic started. Because of this reason, the census data for 2020 used a experimental model rather than previous yaers' survey filling. Thus, I ended up choosing 2019's data as my final project data. Plus, because of COVID, there are many Americans start to work from home, which would somehow affect my intention for this project.
